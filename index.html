Replacement Schedule

Keeping your CPAP equipment in optimal condition requires regular replacement of parts. Please note: the following schedule is a general suggestion compiled from publicly available resources and may not cover all scenarios. Your specific replacement needs may vary based on your machine, mask type, insurance requirements and clinician guidance.

ComponentSuggested IntervalReason / Notes
CPAP machine (device)Every 5 yearsTypical useful lifetime; replace earlier if malfunctioning
Nasal mask cushionsEvery 14 daysMaintain seal; skin oils degrade material
Nasal pillows mask cushionsEvery 14 daysMaintain seal; skin oils degrade material
Full face maskEvery 1 monthReplace regularly for hygiene and effective therapy
Mask frame systemEvery 3 monthsManufacturer & supplier guideline
Headgear / strapsEvery 6 monthsLoss of elasticity or fit; hygiene
Air filtersEvery 14 daysPrevent clogging; maintain airflow
Air tubingEvery 3 monthsPrevent leaks and tears
Humidifier water tubEvery 6 monthsPrevent bacteria and mineral buildup

These intervals are suggestions from various online resources and are not comprehensive. Always consult your provider and confirm with your specific insurance plan for personalized replacement guidance.

Insurance Coverage Guidance

Insurance requirements may vary. Generally, insurance providers require documented compliant use of your CPAP machine (e.g., at least 21 days of use for 4+ hours per night within a 30‑day period during the first 90 days). Some plans may also require an in‑person follow‑up visit within the first 90 days.
